Wayside lubrication apparatus for railroad track wherein metered charges of lubricant are delivered to points spaced at intervals along the gage sides of the heads of the rails, and systems for and methods of monitoring the apparatus at a location remote from the site thereof.
Claims What is claimed is: 1. Railroad rail head lubrication apparatus comprising: an elongate applicator for attachment to a railroad rail on the inside of the rail extending lengthwise of the rail, said applicator having a lubricant flow divider comprising a group of lubricant metering devices, each of said metering devices comprising a divider valve operable in response to delivery thereto of lubricant under pressure to deliver a metered charge of lubricant and to become charged for a subsequent delivery of a metered charge, the metered charges delivered by said devices being delivered to points spaced at intervals along the length of the applicator with the delivery such as to apply the delivered charges to the inside of the head of the rail to which the applicator is attached. 2.
